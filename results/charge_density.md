# Charge Density and Bonding Analysis

## Objective

Analyze the bonding nature of silicon using real-space charge density and confirm the covalent bonding picture.

---

## Key Results

- Charge density is not localized only on atoms
- Continuous electron density observed between neighboring atoms
- Directional bonding consistent with tetrahedral geometry

---

## Governing Concept

Charge density is defined as:

ρ(r)

It represents the spatial distribution of electrons in the system.

---

## Physical Interpretation

### 3D Isosurface

The charge density forms a continuous surface that extends between atoms.

This indicates:
- electrons are shared between atoms
- bonding is covalent

---

### 2D Slice (Bond Plane)

The 2D slice through a Si–Si bond shows:

- high density near atomic centers
- continuous density between atoms
- no depletion region between neighbors

This confirms:
- directional bonding
- electron sharing along bond directions

---

## Remarks

- The isosurface shape depends on the chosen isovalue
- The slice plane must pass through a bond to reveal bonding clearly
- Total charge density includes all electrons, not only bonding electrons

## Connection to Electronic Structure

The charge density results complement PDOS:

- PDOS → orbital hybridization (s + p)
- Charge density → real-space electron sharing

Together, they confirm:

- sp³ hybridization
- covalent bonding in diamond-structure silicon
